# Financial Glossary

## A

**401(k)**: Employer-sponsored retirement plan allowing pre-tax or Roth contributions.

**403(b)**: Retirement plan for employees of nonprofits, schools, and government.

**529 Plan**: Tax-advantaged savings plan for education expenses.

**Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)**: Gross income minus specific deductions (above-the-line).

**Alpha**: Investment return above the benchmark, attributed to manager skill.

**Amortization**: Spreading loan payments over time; principal and interest portions.

**Annual Percentage Rate (APR)**: Yearly interest rate including fees.

**Annual Percentage Yield (APY)**: Effective annual rate accounting for compound interest.

**Annuity**: Insurance contract providing regular payments, often for retirement.

**Appreciation**: Increase in asset value over time.

**Asset Allocation**: Distribution of investments across asset classes (stocks, bonds, etc.).

**Assets Under Management (AUM)**: Total value of investments managed by an advisor or fund.

## B

**Basis Point (bps)**: One-hundredth of a percentage point (0.01%). 100 bps = 1%.

**Bear Market**: Market decline of 20% or more from recent highs.

**Benchmark**: Standard for measuring investment performance (e.g., S&P 500).

**Beneficiary**: Person designated to receive assets upon account holder's death.

**Beta**: Measure of investment volatility relative to the market.

**Blue Chip**: Large, well-established, financially stable company.

**Bond**: Debt security where issuer borrows money and pays interest.

**Bond Ladder**: Strategy of owning bonds with staggered maturities.

**Bull Market**: Extended period of rising stock prices.

**Buyback**: Company repurchasing its own shares from the market.

## C

**Capital Gain**: Profit from selling an asset for more than purchase price.

**Capital Loss**: Loss from selling an asset for less than purchase price.

**Cash Flow**: Money moving in and out of an investment or business.

**CD (Certificate of Deposit)**: Bank deposit with fixed term and interest rate.

**Compound Interest**: Interest earned on both principal and accumulated interest.

**Consumer Price Index (CPI)**: Measure of inflation based on consumer goods prices.

**Correction**: Market decline of 10-20% from recent highs.

**Cost Basis**: Original value of an asset for tax purposes.

**Coupon Rate**: Annual interest rate paid by a bond.

**Credit Rating**: Assessment of creditworthiness (e.g., AAA, BBB).

**Credit Utilization**: Percentage of available credit being used.

**Current Yield**: Annual income divided by current price.

## D

**Deductible**: Amount you pay before insurance coverage begins.

**Depreciation**: Decrease in asset value over time; or tax deduction for business assets.

**Diversification**: Spreading investments to reduce risk.

**Dividend**: Distribution of company profits to shareholders.

**Dividend Yield**: Annual dividend divided by stock price.

**Dollar-Cost Averaging**: Investing fixed amounts at regular intervals.

**Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA)**: Index of 30 large U.S. companies.

**Duration**: Measure of bond price sensitivity to interest rate changes.

## E

**Earnings Per Share (EPS)**: Company profit divided by outstanding shares.

**EBITDA**: Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization.

**Efficient Market Hypothesis**: Theory that stock prices reflect all available information.

**Equity**: Ownership stake; stock; or home value minus mortgage.

**Estate Tax**: Tax on assets transferred at death.

**ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)**: Fund trading on exchange like a stock.

**Ex-Dividend Date**: Date after which new buyers don't receive declared dividend.

**Expense Ratio**: Annual fee charged by funds, expressed as percentage of assets.

## F

**FDIC**: Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, insuring bank deposits.

**Fiduciary**: Legal obligation to act in client's best interest.

**Fixed Income**: Investments providing regular, predictable income (bonds).

**Fundamental Analysis**: Evaluating securities based on financial and economic data.

**Futures**: Contracts to buy/sell assets at predetermined future date and price.

## G

**Gross Income**: Total income before deductions and taxes.

**Growth Investing**: Strategy focusing on companies with high growth potential.

## H

**Hedge**: Investment to reduce risk of adverse price movements.

**High-Yield Bond**: Lower-rated bond offering higher interest (junk bond).

**HSA**: Health Savings Account with triple tax advantage.

## I

**Index**: Statistical measure of market or sector performance.

**Index Fund**: Fund that tracks a market index.

**Inflation**: General increase in prices; decrease in purchasing power.

**Initial Public Offering (IPO)**: Company's first sale of stock to public.

**Interest Rate**: Cost of borrowing money or return on lending.

**IRA**: Individual Retirement Account with tax advantages.

## J-K

**Junk Bond**: High-yield bond with below investment-grade rating.

## L

**Large-Cap**: Company with market capitalization over $10 billion.

**Leverage**: Using borrowed money to increase investment potential.

**Liability**: Debt or financial obligation.

**Limit Order**: Order to buy/sell at specific price or better.

**Liquidity**: Ease of converting investment to cash without significant loss.

**Load**: Sales charge on mutual fund purchase (front-end) or sale (back-end).

## M

**Margin**: Borrowed money from broker to purchase investments.

**Market Capitalization**: Total value of company's outstanding shares.

**Market Order**: Order to buy/sell immediately at current price.

**Maturity**: Date when bond principal is repaid.

**Mid-Cap**: Company with market capitalization between $2-10 billion.

**Money Market**: Low-risk, short-term debt investments.

**Moving Average**: Average price over specified period, used in technical analysis.

**Municipal Bond**: Bond issued by state or local government, often tax-exempt.

**Mutual Fund**: Investment pooling money from multiple investors.

## N

**NASDAQ**: Electronic stock exchange, technology-heavy.

**NAV (Net Asset Value)**: Fund's per-share value.

**Net Worth**: Assets minus liabilities.

**NYSE**: New York Stock Exchange, largest by market cap.

## O

**Options**: Contracts giving right to buy (call) or sell (put) at set price.

## P

**P/E Ratio**: Price-to-earnings ratio; stock price divided by EPS.

**Par Value**: Face value of a bond.

**Passive Investing**: Strategy of matching market returns through index funds.

**Pension**: Employer-funded retirement plan providing defined benefit.

**Portfolio**: Collection of investments held by individual or institution.

**Premium**: Bond price above par; or insurance payment.

**Price-to-Book (P/B)**: Stock price divided by book value per share.

**Principal**: Original investment amount; or loan balance.

## Q

**Qualified Dividend**: Dividend eligible for lower tax rates.

## R

**Rebalancing**: Adjusting portfolio back to target allocation.

**REIT**: Real Estate Investment Trust, company owning income properties.

**Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)**: Mandatory retirement account withdrawals.

**Return on Equity (ROE)**: Net income divided by shareholders' equity.

**Risk Tolerance**: Ability and willingness to endure investment losses.

**Rollover**: Moving retirement funds between accounts.

**Roth IRA/401(k)**: Account with after-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals.

**Russell 2000**: Index of 2,000 small-cap U.S. stocks.

## S

**S&P 500**: Index of 500 large U.S. companies.

**SEC**: Securities and Exchange Commission, market regulator.

**Sector**: Industry group (technology, healthcare, financials, etc.).

**Securities**: Tradable financial assets (stocks, bonds).

**Sharpe Ratio**: Risk-adjusted return measure.

**Short Selling**: Borrowing shares to sell, hoping to buy back lower.

**Small-Cap**: Company with market capitalization under $2 billion.

**Standard Deviation**: Measure of volatility/risk.

**Stock**: Ownership share in a company.

**Stock Split**: Dividing existing shares into more shares.

**Stop-Loss Order**: Order to sell if price falls to specified level.

## T

**Target-Date Fund**: Fund adjusting allocation based on retirement year.

**Tax-Deferred**: Investment where taxes are postponed until withdrawal.

**Tax-Loss Harvesting**: Selling losers to offset gains for tax purposes.

**Technical Analysis**: Studying price patterns and charts to predict movements.

**Term Life Insurance**: Life insurance for specified period.

**Treasury Bond/Note/Bill**: U.S. government debt securities.

**Trust**: Legal arrangement for managing assets for beneficiaries.

## U-V

**Underwriting**: Process of evaluating risk for insurance or securities issuance.

**Value Investing**: Strategy of finding underpriced investments.

**Vesting**: Process of earning rights to employer contributions.

**VIX**: Volatility Index, "fear gauge" measuring expected market volatility.

**Volatility**: Degree of price fluctuation; measure of risk.

## W-X-Y-Z

**Wash Sale**: Selling security at loss and repurchasing within 30 days (disallowed for tax purposes).

**Whole Life Insurance**: Permanent life insurance with cash value.

**Yield**: Income return on investment, expressed as percentage.

**Yield Curve**: Graph showing interest rates across different maturities.

**Yield to Maturity (YTM)**: Total return if bond held to maturity.

**Zero-Coupon Bond**: Bond sold at discount, paying face value at maturity.
