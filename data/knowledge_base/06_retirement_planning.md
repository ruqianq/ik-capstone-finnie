# Retirement Planning Guide

## The Retirement Planning Process

Retirement planning involves setting retirement income goals and making decisions to achieve them. The earlier you start, the more time compound growth works in your favor.

## Key Retirement Concepts

### The 4% Rule
A guideline for sustainable retirement withdrawals:
- Withdraw 4% of portfolio in year one
- Adjust for inflation each subsequent year
- Historically provides 30-year portfolio survival
- Example: $1 million portfolio = $40,000/year initial withdrawal

### Replacement Ratio
How much pre-retirement income you need:
- General target: 70-80% of pre-retirement income
- Lower expenses in retirement (no commute, mortgage paid off)
- But healthcare costs often increase
- Calculate your specific needs

### Retirement Savings Milestones
General guidelines by age:
- **30**: 1x annual salary saved
- **40**: 3x annual salary saved
- **50**: 6x annual salary saved
- **60**: 8x annual salary saved
- **67**: 10x annual salary saved

## Retirement Accounts

### Employer-Sponsored Plans

#### 401(k) Plans
- **Contribution Limit (2024)**: $23,000 ($30,500 if 50+)
- **Employer Match**: Free money - always contribute enough to get full match
- **Traditional 401(k)**: Pre-tax contributions, tax-deferred growth, taxed at withdrawal
- **Roth 401(k)**: After-tax contributions, tax-free growth and qualified withdrawals
- **Vesting**: May need years of service to own employer contributions

#### 403(b) Plans
- For employees of nonprofits, schools, government
- Similar rules to 401(k)
- Same contribution limits

#### 457(b) Plans
- For state/local government and some nonprofits
- Same contribution limits as 401(k)
- Can contribute to both 457(b) AND 401(k)/403(b)
- No 10% early withdrawal penalty

### Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs)

#### Traditional IRA
- **Contribution Limit (2024)**: $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+)
- **Tax Deduction**: May be limited based on income if covered by workplace plan
- **Growth**: Tax-deferred
- **Withdrawals**: Taxed as ordinary income
- **RMDs**: Required starting at age 73

#### Roth IRA
- **Contribution Limit (2024)**: $7,000 ($8,000 if 50+)
- **Income Limits**: MAGI up to $161,000 single / $240,000 married
- **Contributions**: After-tax (no deduction)
- **Growth**: Tax-free
- **Qualified Withdrawals**: Tax-free (after age 59Â½ and 5-year rule)
- **No RMDs**: During owner's lifetime

#### Backdoor Roth IRA
For high earners above Roth income limits:
1. Contribute to Traditional IRA (non-deductible)
2. Convert to Roth IRA
3. Pay tax on any gains
4. Beware of pro-rata rule if you have existing traditional IRA funds

### Self-Employed Retirement Accounts

#### SEP IRA
- Contribution up to 25% of compensation (max $69,000 in 2024)
- Simple to set up and administer
- Only employer contributions allowed

#### Solo 401(k)
- For self-employed with no employees (except spouse)
- Employee + employer contributions up to $69,000 ($76,500 if 50+)
- Roth option available

#### SIMPLE IRA
- For businesses with 100 or fewer employees
- Employee contribution up to $16,000 ($19,500 if 50+)
- Employer must contribute (match or 2% of pay)

## Social Security

### Basics
- Funded by payroll taxes (FICA)
- Need 40 credits (10 years of work) to qualify
- Benefits based on highest 35 earning years

### Claiming Ages
- **62**: Earliest age, permanently reduced benefits (~30% less)
- **Full Retirement Age (FRA)**: 66-67 depending on birth year
- **70**: Maximum benefits (~32% more than FRA)
- Each year you delay past FRA increases benefits ~8%

### Maximizing Benefits
- Work at least 35 years to avoid zeros in calculation
- Delay claiming if you can afford to
- Consider spousal and survivor benefits
- Coordinate with spouse for optimal household benefits

### Spousal Benefits
- Can claim up to 50% of spouse's FRA benefit
- Must be at least 62 or caring for eligible child
- Ex-spouses may qualify if married 10+ years

## Retirement Income Strategies

### The Bucket Strategy
Divide portfolio into time-based buckets:
1. **Bucket 1 (1-2 years)**: Cash, money market for immediate needs
2. **Bucket 2 (3-10 years)**: Bonds, stable income investments
3. **Bucket 3 (10+ years)**: Stocks for long-term growth

### Income Floor Strategy
Guarantee essential expenses with guaranteed income:
- Social Security covers base needs
- Consider annuities for additional guaranteed income
- Invest remaining assets for growth

### Systematic Withdrawal Strategy
Withdraw fixed percentage from portfolio:
- 4% rule or dynamic variations
- Adjust based on market performance
- Requires discipline and monitoring

## Healthcare in Retirement

### Medicare
- Eligible at 65 (enroll 3 months before)
- **Part A**: Hospital coverage (usually premium-free)
- **Part B**: Medical coverage (~$175/month in 2024)
- **Part D**: Prescription drug coverage
- **Medigap**: Supplemental coverage for gaps

### Health Savings Accounts (HSA)
Triple tax advantage:
1. Tax-deductible contributions
2. Tax-free growth
3. Tax-free withdrawals for medical expenses
- After 65, can withdraw for any purpose (taxed as income)
- Good retirement medical expense fund

### Long-Term Care
Consider planning for:
- Nursing home costs ($8,000-12,000/month)
- Home health care
- Long-term care insurance options
- Self-insurance if sufficient assets

## Retirement Planning Mistakes

1. **Starting Too Late**: Every decade delayed costs significantly
2. **Not Maximizing Match**: Leaving free money on the table
3. **Ignoring Fees**: High fees compound negatively over decades
4. **Too Conservative Too Early**: Young savers need growth
5. **Underestimating Longevity**: Plan for 30+ year retirement
6. **Ignoring Inflation**: Purchasing power erodes over time
7. **Claiming Social Security Early**: Often leaves money on table
8. **Underestimating Healthcare Costs**: Can be largest expense
9. **Forgetting About Taxes**: Plan for tax-efficient withdrawals
10. **No Written Plan**: Winging it doesn't work

## Retirement Readiness Checklist

### 5+ Years Before Retirement
- [ ] Calculate retirement income needs
- [ ] Maximize retirement contributions
- [ ] Pay off high-interest debt
- [ ] Review asset allocation
- [ ] Consider catch-up contributions

### 1-2 Years Before Retirement
- [ ] Create detailed retirement budget
- [ ] Develop withdrawal strategy
- [ ] Understand Medicare enrollment
- [ ] Plan for healthcare gap (if retiring before 65)
- [ ] Review Social Security statement

### At Retirement
- [ ] Set up income streams
- [ ] Consolidate accounts if helpful
- [ ] Update beneficiaries
- [ ] Create estate planning documents
- [ ] Enroll in Medicare (if 65+)
