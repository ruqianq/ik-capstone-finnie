# Real Estate Investing Guide

## Why Invest in Real Estate?

Real estate offers unique benefits as an asset class, including income generation, appreciation potential, tax advantages, and portfolio diversification.

## Types of Real Estate Investments

### Direct Ownership

#### Primary Residence
- Not strictly an investment (you need somewhere to live)
- Builds equity over time
- Mortgage interest deduction (if itemizing)
- Capital gains exclusion ($250K single, $500K married)

#### Rental Properties
- Generate monthly income from tenants
- Appreciation over time
- Tax deductions for expenses
- Active involvement required (or hire property manager)

#### Fix-and-Flip
- Buy undervalued properties
- Renovate to increase value
- Sell for profit
- Requires expertise, capital, and time
- Higher risk than long-term holding

### Indirect Real Estate Investment

#### REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts)
Companies that own, operate, or finance real estate:
- **Equity REITs**: Own physical properties
- **Mortgage REITs**: Hold mortgages and loans
- **Hybrid REITs**: Combination of both

Benefits:
- Liquidity (trade on exchanges)
- Diversification across many properties
- Professional management
- Required to distribute 90% of income as dividends
- No property management hassles

#### Real Estate Mutual Funds and ETFs
- Own diversified portfolio of REITs
- Even more diversified than single REIT
- Low minimum investment
- Professional management

#### Real Estate Crowdfunding
- Invest in specific properties or portfolios
- Lower minimums than direct ownership
- Less liquid than REITs
- Platforms: Fundrise, CrowdStreet, RealtyMogul

#### Real Estate Syndications
- Pool money with other investors
- Professional sponsor manages property
- Typically accredited investors only
- Less liquid, but potential for higher returns

## Evaluating Rental Properties

### Key Metrics

#### Cash Flow
Monthly income minus expenses:
```
Cash Flow = Rent - (Mortgage + Taxes + Insurance + Maintenance + Vacancy + Management)
```
Positive cash flow essential for long-term success.

#### Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate)
```
Cap Rate = Net Operating Income รท Property Value
```
- Higher cap rate = higher potential return (and often higher risk)
- Typical range: 4-10%
- Market dependent

#### Cash-on-Cash Return
```
Cash-on-Cash = Annual Cash Flow รท Total Cash Invested
```
- Measures return on your actual cash investment
- Target: 8-12% or higher

#### 1% Rule
Monthly rent should be at least 1% of purchase price:
- $200,000 property should rent for $2,000/month
- Quick screening tool, not definitive
- Works in some markets, not others

#### 50% Rule
Operating expenses typically run 50% of rent:
- If rent is $2,000, expect $1,000 for expenses
- Helps estimate cash flow quickly
- Excludes mortgage payment

## Financing Rental Properties

### Conventional Mortgages
- 15-25% down payment typically required for investment
- Higher interest rates than primary residence
- Strong credit required (700+)
- Limit on number of properties (usually 10)

### Portfolio Loans
- Held by lender (not sold to Fannie/Freddie)
- More flexible requirements
- May be higher rates
- Good for investors with many properties

### Commercial Loans
- For larger properties (5+ units)
- Based on property income, not personal income
- Shorter terms (5-10 years), then refinance
- Larger down payments

### Creative Financing
- Seller financing
- Lease options
- Subject-to existing financing
- Private money lenders
- Requires more expertise and caution

## Tax Benefits of Real Estate

### Deductions
- Mortgage interest
- Property taxes
- Insurance
- Maintenance and repairs
- Property management fees
- Travel to manage property
- Professional services (accountant, attorney)

### Depreciation
- Deduct cost of building over 27.5 years (residential)
- Reduces taxable income without cash expense
- Example: $275,000 building = $10,000 annual deduction
- Recaptured at sale (25% rate)

### 1031 Exchange
Defer capital gains by reinvesting in "like-kind" property:
- 45 days to identify replacement property
- 180 days to close
- Must be equal or greater value
- Can defer indefinitely if continue exchanging

### Qualified Opportunity Zones
Invest capital gains in designated areas:
- Defer original gain
- Reduce gain if held 5+ years
- Exclude appreciation if held 10+ years

### Evaluating REITs

#### Funds From Operations (FFO)
- Net income + depreciation - gains from sales
- Better measure than EPS for REITs
- P/FFO ratio similar to P/E

#### Adjusted FFO (AFFO)
- FFO minus maintenance capital expenditures
- More conservative measure
- Closer to actual cash available for dividends

#### Dividend Yield
- Annual dividend รท share price
- REITs typically yield 3-8%
- Compare to sector averages

#### Payout Ratio
- Dividends รท FFO
- Below 100% is sustainable
- Much above 100% is concerning

## Real Estate in Your Portfolio

### Allocation Guidelines
- Typical recommendation: 5-20% of portfolio
- Can be higher for income-focused investors
- Include in retirement accounts for tax efficiency

### Diversification Within Real Estate
- Multiple property types
- Geographic diversity
- Mix of REITs and direct ownership if possible
- Public and private real estate

### Rebalancing
- Real estate can appreciate faster than other assets
- Rebalance periodically to maintain target allocation
- Consider tax implications when selling

## Risks of Real Estate Investing

### Direct Ownership Risks
- Vacancy and tenant issues
- Property damage
- Market decline
- Interest rate changes
- Illiquidity
- Capital intensive
- Time commitment

### REIT Risks
- Interest rate sensitivity
- Sector-specific downturns
- Economic cycles
- Management quality
- Dividend cuts possible

### Mitigation Strategies
- Diversification across properties/sectors
- Adequate reserves for vacancies and repairs
- Quality tenants (thorough screening)
- Appropriate insurance
- Conservative leverage
- Long-term holding period

## Getting Started

### First Steps for Direct Investing
1. Build emergency fund and reduce debt
2. Save for down payment (20-25%)
3. Learn your market thoroughly
4. Build team (realtor, lender, contractor, property manager)
5. Analyze many deals before buying
6. Start with one property, learn, then expand

### First Steps for REIT Investing
1. Determine allocation to real estate
2. Choose between individual REITs or REIT funds
3. Consider tax placement (retirement vs. taxable accounts)
4. Research options (diversified vs. sector-specific)
5. Start position and add over time

## Common Mistakes

1. **Not running the numbers** before purchasing
2. **Underestimating expenses** (vacancies, repairs, management)
3. **Buying in unfamiliar markets**
4. **Overleveraging** (too much debt)
5. **Poor tenant screening**
6. **Neglecting insurance**
7. **Not having reserves** for unexpected costs
8. **Emotional purchasing** (falling in love with property)
9. **Ignoring location fundamentals**
10. **Expecting quick profits**
