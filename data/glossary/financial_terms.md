# Financial Glossary

## Stocks
A stock (also known as equity) is a type of security that signifies proportionate ownership in the issuing corporation. When you buy stock, you become a shareholder and own a portion of the company.

## Bonds
A bond is a fixed-income instrument that represents a loan made by an investor to a borrower (typically corporate or governmental). Bonds are used to finance projects and operations, and they pay interest over time.

## ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)
An ETF is a type of investment fund that tracks an index, commodity, bonds, or a basket of assets. ETFs trade on stock exchanges similar to stocks.

## Mutual Fund
A mutual fund pools money from many investors to purchase securities like stocks, bonds, and other assets. Mutual funds are managed by professional portfolio managers.

## 401(k)
A 401(k) is an employer-sponsored retirement savings plan that allows employees to save and invest a portion of their paycheck before taxes are taken out.

## IRA (Individual Retirement Account)
An IRA is a retirement savings account that provides tax advantages for retirement savings. There are traditional IRAs and Roth IRAs with different tax treatments.

## Asset Allocation
Asset allocation is an investment strategy that aims to balance risk and reward by dividing a portfolio among different asset categories such as stocks, bonds, and cash.

## Diversification
Diversification is a risk management strategy that mixes a wide variety of investments within a portfolio to reduce exposure to any single asset or risk.

## Market Capitalization
Market capitalization (market cap) is the total market value of a company's outstanding shares of stock. It's calculated by multiplying the stock price by the number of shares.

## P/E Ratio
The price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio measures a company's current share price relative to its per-share earnings. It helps investors determine the market value of a stock.

## Dividend
A dividend is a distribution of a portion of a company's earnings to its shareholders. Dividends can be paid in cash or additional shares of stock.

## Bull Market
A bull market refers to a market condition in which prices are rising or are expected to rise. It's typically characterized by optimism and investor confidence.

## Bear Market
A bear market is a condition in which securities prices fall 20% or more from recent highs amid widespread pessimism and negative investor sentiment.

## Index Fund
An index fund is a type of mutual fund or ETF designed to track the performance of a specific market index, such as the S&P 500.

## Risk Tolerance
Risk tolerance is the degree of variability in investment returns that an investor is willing to withstand. It depends on factors like age, income, and financial goals.
